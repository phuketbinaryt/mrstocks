# How to read a candidate card

Each candidate on the dashboard is a single tile packing the entire pre-market read of a symbol into one glanceable card. Once you know what each field is, you can triage a 60-name scan in under a minute. Here's the anatomy.

## Top row: symbol + state badge

The largest text on the card is the ticker. The colored chip below is the **setup state** — one of NARROW, WIDE SNAPBACK, WIDE EXTENSION, MEDIUM. State chips are color-coded so you can spot the distribution of setup types at a glance without reading individual labels.

State color conventions (subject to design refinement):

- NARROW — green
- WIDE SNAPBACK — amber
- WIDE EXTENSION — red
- MEDIUM — neutral

## Score

A numeric value from 0 to 100. The score is a composite of distance-to-20MA-in-ATR, range tightness, slope, gap behavior, and liquidity — weighted by the scanner's internal model. **Higher is more interesting**, not more bullish. A score of 85 means "this candidate is in the top 15% of the day's scan by the scanner's ranking"; it says nothing about direction. Use score to rank within state — a 85-score NARROW is more notable than a 50-score NARROW.

## MA distance (ATR)

The number of ATRs the current close is from the 20-day moving average. Roughly:

- `0.0 – 0.5` — at the MA. Pairs with NARROW.
- `0.5 – 1.5` — modestly extended.
- `1.5 – 3.0` — WIDE territory.
- `> 3.0` — extreme. Often a recent gap or news event.

ATR is the 14-period Average True Range — a volatility-normalized measure. 2 ATRs from MA means roughly two "typical days of movement" from the MA. That makes the number comparable across symbols of very different price levels (a $400 stock and a $40 stock both at 1.5 ATR are similarly stretched).

## Gap %

Today's overnight gap as a percent of yesterday's close. A 3% gap on a typical name is large; a 0.2% gap is noise. Used both as a feature in the score and as a sanity check. A NARROW name that gapped 4% overnight is no longer truly NARROW for today's purposes — be cautious.

## Slope

The 10-day slope of the 20MA, normalized. Roughly:

- `> +1` — strong uptrend
- `0 to +1` — mild uptrend
- `0 to -1` — mild downtrend
- `< -1` — strong downtrend

Slope tells you which side of the trade has trend tailwind. NARROW + positive slope + Lower #2 zone is a long-bias coil. NARROW + negative slope + Upper #2 zone is a short-bias coil. Same setup, opposite plays.

## Watch direction

A small arrow indicator (where applicable) showing whether the scanner expects the directional break to favor up or down based on the combination of all features above. This is a hint, not a directive — your chart read is the final word.

## Prior45 position

A short text label like "L2" (Lower #2), "U3" (Upper #3), or "IN" (Inside). Tap the card to open the per-symbol page where the full Prior45 zone band is rendered visually. See [Prior45 zones](/education/prior45-zones) for the meaning of each band.

## Putting it together

A high-conviction card looks something like: **NARROW, score 82, MA dist 0.3, gap 0.1%, slope +1.2, U2.** Translation: a tight coil right at its MA, no overnight gap, in a healthy uptrend, near recent highs. That's a setup worth opening a chart for.

A pass looks like: **MEDIUM, score 45, MA dist 1.1, gap 2.8%, slope -0.4, IN.** Translation: mid-range, mid-distance from MA, gapped overnight, weak trend, middle of the 45-day range. Nothing actionable.

Practice triage on the first 10 cards of any morning's scan and you'll be fluent within a week.
