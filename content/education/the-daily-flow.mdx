# The daily flow

MR/STOCKS fits into a short, repeatable morning routine. The scanner does the heavy lifting before the open; your job is to triage its output and wait for confirmation. Here's the loop.

## 1. Check the scan before the open

The scanner runs around **09:15 NY** and freezes the previous RTH session's state. By the time you open the dashboard, today's ranked list — roughly 500 candidates — is already there. Glance at the distribution: how many NARROW names, how stretched is the tape, is anything sitting in a #3 zone.

## 2. Filter to what you trade

Use the three filter rows at the top of the dashboard:

- **STATE** — narrow to the setups you want (most days, start with NARROW).
- **PRIOR45** — narrow to a zone (e.g. Upper #1 for continuation longs, #3 for reversals only).
- **LIST** — restrict to one of your own watchlists if you trade a focused universe.

Each filter row has a `?` help dot that links back to the article explaining it.

## 3. Identify the A+ setups

The strongest reads are usually **NARROW, high score, clean location, and a directional watch label** — a coil sitting just above or below the cluster with a `LONG · ABOVE CLUSTER` or `SHORT · BELOW CLUSTER` bias. Pair the state with the Prior45 zone: continuation in #1, caution in #2, reversal-only in #3.

## 4. Wait for the opening 2-minute candle

This is the part that separates a watch from a trade. The watch direction is **conditional** — it goes live only when the first regular-session 2-minute candle confirms it:

- **Long** only if that candle opens above both MAs.
- **Short** only if it opens below both MAs.

## 5. Act

Once the open confirms, the [opening playbook](/education/the-opening-playbook) covers the exact entry, stop, target, and max-hold rules. If the open does not confirm your bias, you simply pass — there was no trade.

<Callout tone="info">
Save the names you like to a watchlist and set an alert. Tomorrow's scan will ping you when a matching setup appears, so you can run this loop in a couple of minutes.
</Callout>
