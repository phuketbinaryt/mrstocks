# The Prior45 zone system

The **Prior45 zone band** is a horizontal strip on each symbol detail page that divides the prior-45-session price range into seven labelled regions. The symbol's current closing price sits in one of them, and that placement materially changes what the setup *means*. Same setup state, different zone, different thesis.

## How the band is built

Take the highest high and lowest low of the prior 45 daily candles. That's the outer envelope. Inside that envelope, three bands are drawn above the 22-session midpoint and three below it. The midpoint zone itself is labelled **Inside**.

- **Upper #1** — just above midpoint, still well within range
- **Upper #2** — into the upper third of the 45-day range
- **Upper #3** — at or near the 45-day high
- **Inside** — the middle band centered on the 45-day midpoint
- **Lower #1** — just below midpoint
- **Lower #2** — into the lower third
- **Lower #3** — at or near the 45-day low

The band is a *position* indicator, not a momentum one. A symbol can sit in Upper #3 for weeks (strong trend) or arrive there in two days (extension).

## How the zone changes thesis

The zone tells you **where in the prior range the next move would happen from.** This affects R:R asymmetrically.

### NARROW in Lower #2 / Lower #3
Coiling near the lows. If it expands up, you're catching the move *from* a multi-week low — long R:R is excellent because the prior low is a hard, recent reference for the stop. If it expands down, you're shorting a name already at the low — riskier, lower hit rate.

### NARROW in Upper #2 / Upper #3
Coiling near the highs. The mirror image. Long breakouts here are often immediately retraced because there's no nearby support. Short setups in this zone are higher-conviction *if* the broader market is also extended.

### WIDE SNAPBACK in Upper #3
Stretched above 20MA AND near 45-day high. The mean-reversion thesis is sharpest here — the symbol has nowhere left to go on the upside in the short term. Highest-probability fade setups live in this combo.

### WIDE SNAPBACK in Inside
Symbol is multiple ATRs from MA but only mid-range over 45 days. This is a less reliable mean-reversion setup because the symbol *has* recently been higher/lower than where it sits now — the "stretched" reading is shorter-term.

### Any state in Inside
The middle zone is the lowest-information zone. Treat any candidate sitting in Inside with one extra grain of salt; the scanner ranks them on other features (slope, score, gap behavior) and they can still be tradeable, but the zone isn't doing meaningful filtering work.

## Using zones in alert rules

Most members configure alert rules that target *specific zone combinations* — for example, "fire on NARROW + Lower #2 or Lower #3, min score 70, my mega-cap watchlist." This makes the alert queue actionable instead of noisy. Configure your zone selections on the `/alerts/new` page.
