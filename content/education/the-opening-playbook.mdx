# The opening playbook

</Callout>

The scanner gives you a pre-open picture and a conditional bias. The **opening playbook** is the rule set that turns that bias into a trade, anchored to the **first regular-session 2-minute candle**.

## The trigger candle

The scanner runs pre-open (≈09:15 NY) and freezes the **previous RTH session's** state. The entry decision waits for the **first regular-session 2-minute candle**:

- **Long** only if that candle **opens above both MAs**.
- **Short** only if that candle **opens below both MAs**.

If the open doesn't satisfy the side your watch label expected, there is no trade. The bias was conditional; the open is the condition.

## Entry, stop, target

Once the first 2-minute candle defines the side:

- **Entry** — 1 cent beyond the first candle's **high** (long) or **low** (short).
- **Stop** — 1 cent beyond the **opposite** side of that same first candle.
- **Target** — **1R** by default (one multiple of the entry-to-stop risk).
- **Max hold** — **12 bars**. If the target hasn't filled by then, the trade comes off.

A more transcript-faithful variant additionally requires the **first candle's color to agree** with the trade direction (green for long, red for short).

## The mindset

The scanner is **advisory**: it surfaces the pre-open picture, and the actual entry always needs the opening event to confirm. You are not predicting the break — you are reacting to it within a tightly defined first-candle structure.
