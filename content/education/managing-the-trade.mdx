# Managing the trade

<Callout tone="caution">
Draft — pending trader review. The rules below are exactly those documented in the scanner logic; position-sizing and scaling are deliberately left out because they are not specified there. Confirm the discretionary nuances with the desk.
</Callout>

Once the opening trigger has you in, management is simple by design — the rules are fixed up front so there are no mid-trade decisions to second-guess.

## Where the stop sits

The stop is placed at the entry: **1 cent beyond the opposite side of the first 2-minute candle**. For a long, that's 1¢ below the first candle's low; for a short, 1¢ above its high. The first candle defines both your entry and your risk, so your **R** (risk unit) is the distance from entry to that stop.

## The target

The default target is **1R** — one multiple of that entry-to-stop distance. The trade is sized in R, not in fixed dollar amounts, so a tighter first candle means a tighter stop and a closer 1R target.

## The 12-bar max hold

If price hasn't reached the 1R target, the trade does **not** sit open indefinitely. There is a **12-bar maximum hold**: after 12 bars, the position comes off regardless of where it is. This caps time-in-trade and stops a stalled opening setup from drifting into the middle of the session.

## The color-agreement variant

The more transcript-faithful variant adds one condition at entry: the **first candle's color must agree with the trade direction** — green for a long, red for a short. It's a stricter filter on which opening triggers you act on; it doesn't change the stop, target, or max-hold once you're in.

<Callout tone="info">
The documented rule set is: entry 1¢ beyond the first-candle extreme, stop 1¢ beyond the opposite extreme, 1R target, 12-bar max hold, optional color agreement. Anything beyond that — scaling, trailing, partial exits, position sizing — is not part of the scanner's spec and is left to your own risk management.
</Callout>
