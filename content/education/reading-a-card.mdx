# How to read a candidate card

Each candidate is a single tile packing the entire pre-open read of a symbol into one glanceable card. Once you know what each field is, you can triage a 500-name scan quickly. Here's the anatomy, on a real card.

<AnnotatedCard />

## The fields, one by one

- **Ticker** — the symbol. Click the card to open its detail page with the full Prior45 zone band.
- **State badge** — the setup state. Read it first; it sets the mental model. See [the six states](/education/states-explained).
- **Watch direction** — the conditional bias chip (`LONG · ABOVE CLUSTER`, `SHORT · BELOW CLUSTER`, or `WAIT · NO TRIGGER`). It's a bias, not a trigger. See [the watch direction](/education/watch-direction).
- **Price** — the last price the scan saw.
- **Gap %** — `(current close − previous RTH close) / previous RTH close × 100`. Green for up, red for down.
- **Score** — the composite 0–100 ranking (state + liquidity + location), not a win probability. See [how the score is built](/education/the-score).

## The metric grid

- **MA dist** — the gap between the moving averages, in **ATR** units. This is what drives the state: `[0.05, 0.75] ATR` is NARROW territory, `≥ 1.50 ATR` is wide.
- **MA dist %** — the same gap expressed as a percent. The state uses whichever of ATR or percent qualifies.
- **ATR** — the volatility unit the distances are measured in. It makes a $400 stock and a $40 stock comparable.
- **Vol $** — average dollar volume. This feeds the liquidity part of the score (`log10` of dollar volume, capped at +20).
- **Slope fast** — the fast MA's slope in ATR. "Flat enough" for NARROW means `fast_slope ≤ 0.12 ATR` (and `slow_slope ≤ 0.05 ATR`); sloping MAs cost the NARROW score 8 points.
- **Prior45** — the zone position (`Inside`, `Upper #1–3`, `Lower #1–3`). See [Prior45 zones](/education/prior45-zones).

## Putting it together

The sample above reads: **NARROW, score 96, MA dist 0.31 ATR, gap +0.84%, slope fast 0.04, Upper #1, LONG · ABOVE CLUSTER.** A tight, flat coil sitting just above the band in a liquid name, biased long if the open confirms above both MAs. That's a card worth opening a chart for — and then waiting for the opening trigger.
